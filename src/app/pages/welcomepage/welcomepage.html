Application to Set Up a Hong Kong Foreign Trust

Hong Kong trusts, governed by the Trustee Ordinance and common law, offer a robust and flexible framework for wealth management, particularly for high-net-worth individuals. They provide asset protection, tax efficiency, and the ability to structure trusts with perpetual duration, shielding assets from foreign forced heirship rules. Hong Kong's legal system also recognizes and enforces foreign trusts, making it a suitable location for international estate planning.

Key Benefits of a Hong Kong Trust

Asset Protection

While not offering statutory asset protection like some jurisdictions with firewall legislation, Hong Kong common law principles generally uphold the integrity of trust arrangements against creditors and claimants, provided the trust was not established with fraudulent intent.

Tax Efficiency

Hong Kong offers tax advantages, including no capital gains tax and exemptions on foreign-sourced income, making it an attractive jurisdiction for wealth management.

Perpetual Trusts

The Trust Law (Amendment) Ordinance 2013 allows for the establishment of perpetual trusts, enabling long-term wealth preservation across multiple generations.

Privacy

Hong Kong trusts offer a high degree of privacy, with no public registry of trusts or mandatory disclosure of trust documents to the government.

Recognition of Foreign Trusts

Hong Kong courts generally recognize and enforce trusts established under the laws of other jurisdictions.



Key Legal Framework

Trustee Ordinance

This ordinance lays out the fundamental duties and powers of trustees, ensuring the protection of beneficiaries.

Common Law

Hong Kong's legal system is based on English common law, which provides a flexible foundation for trust creation and administration.

Hague Trusts Convention

The Recognition of Trusts Ordinance incorporates the Hague Trusts Convention, further solidifying the enforceability of foreign trusts within Hong Kong.

Recognition of Foreign Trusts

Hong Kong courts generally recognize and enforce trusts established under the laws of other jurisdictions.



Important Considerations

Trust Deed

A trust deed is a crucial document that outlines the terms and conditions of the trust, including the trustee's duties and the beneficiaries' rights.

Trustee

The trustee holds legal ownership of the trust assets and is responsible for managing the trust in accordance with the trust deed and applicable laws.

Beneficiaries

Beneficiaries are the individuals or entities who are entitled to benefit from the trust assets.

Foreign Forced Heirship Rules

Hong Kong trusts are not subject to foreign forced heirship rules, offering settlors greater control over the distribution of their assets.
